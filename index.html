Welcome to St. Francis of Assisi

Mamelodi West Parish

St. Francis of Assisi – Mamelodi West is a thriving parish rooted in faith, service, and community. Inspired by the humility and compassion of our patron saint, we strive to reflect Christ’s love through meaningful worship, acts of kindness, and a deep respect for all of creation.

We are an inclusive and welcoming community, open to all who seek spiritual growth, connection, and purpose. Guided by the teachings of the Gospel and the example of St. Francis, we walk together in faith, supporting one another on the journey toward a more loving and just world.

Mission Statement

"To glorify God by bearing much fruit as authentic disciples of Christ." - John 15:8

Vision Statement

"Embracing God's Love – Serving Our Faithful Community – Stewarding Creation."

This vision encapsulates the church's mission to foster participation and collaboration while reflecting the principles of St. Francis. It provides a guiding framework for church activities and outreach efforts, focusing on the following key elements:

  • Faithful Worship: Creating a meaningful worship environment to deepen connections with God.
  • Discipleship and Spiritual Growth: Offering programs to empower members in living out their faith.
  • Creation Care: Promoting environmental stewardship and sustainability in honor of St. Francis.
  • Diversity and Inclusion: Fostering an inclusive community that values and respects all backgrounds.
  • Equipping Leaders: Developing church leaders who are passionate about service and utilizing their gifts.
  • Community Engagement: Actively serving internal and external communities through kindness and outreach.

This mission and vision work together to inspire the congregation's commitment to living out their faith and engaging meaningfully with the community and the world.

St. Francis' Prayer

MAKE ME AN INSTRUMENT OF YOUR PEACE
Lord, make me an instrument of your peace.
Where there is hatred, let me sow love;
where there is injury, pardon;
where there is doubt, faith;
where there is despair, hope;
where there is darkness, light;
and where there is sadness, joy.

O Divine Master, grant that I may not so much seek
to be consoled as to console;
to be understood as to understand;
to be loved as to love.
For it is in giving that we receive;
it is in pardoning that we are pardoned;
and it is in dying that we are born to eternal life.
AMEN.
