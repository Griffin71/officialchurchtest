Formation of the Organization

The Mothers‚Äô Union (MU) was founded in 1878 by Mary Sumner in Old Alresford, Hampshire, where her husband, George Sumner, served as rector. Inspired by the birth of her first grandchild, Mary was motivated by her belief that mothers held the primary responsibility for raising children in the love of God and that they could achieve this through lives dedicated to prayer.

Mary convened a meeting at the rectory to communicate the purpose of the new society. The enthusiasm and support from those present led to the Bishop of Winchester recognizing the Mothers‚Äô Union as a diocesan organization. This initiative quickly expanded beyond Winchester and into other countries, including South Africa, where it was introduced in the Diocese of Pretoria in 1905. Unfortunately, detailed documentation is lacking regarding its inception at St. Francis. However, by the time the new church in Mamelodi West was laid on 16th February 1964 by Reverend ZH Thema, the Mothers‚Äô Union was already active in St. Francis Church. Today, in 2024, the guild boasts a membership of 67, with 64 members affiliated.

Purpose, Aim, and Objectives of the Mothers‚Äô Union

The core purpose of the Mothers‚Äô Union is to focus on strengthening and preserving marriage and Christian family life. The Society aims to advance the Christian religion, particularly concerning marriage and family life. To achieve this aim, the following objectives have been set:

  • Uphold Christ‚Äôs teachings on the nature of marriage and promote a broader understanding of it.
  • Encourage parents to raise their children in the faith and life of the Church.
  • Maintain a worldwide fellowship of Christians united in prayer, worship, and service.
  • Promote societal conditions that favor stable family life and the protection of children.
  • Assist those whose family life has encountered difficulties.
