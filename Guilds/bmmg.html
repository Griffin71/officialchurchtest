ST. BERNARD MIZEKI MEN’S GUILD

Background

The St. Bernard Mizeki Men’s Guild at St. Francis Anglican Church was founded in 1990 under the leadership of Reverend Sondiyazi. Alongside three dedicated church members—Mr. Dyantyi, Mrs. Papa, and Mrs. Shabangu—the guild was established with the mission of spreading the Gospel and enhancing spiritual growth. The founders emphasized the importance of service to others, encouraging active participation among members in church life and promoting Christian values within their local communities.

Aims and Objectives

  • Weekly Worship: To meet regularly for prayer and worship services, fostering a deeper faith in God.
  • Support: To visit the sick, the sorrowful, and those in need, offering comfort and assistance to individuals facing challenges or hardships.
  • Patronal Festival: To uphold and actively participate in the commemoration of the St. Bernard Mizeki Patronal Festival, celebrated annually on June 18.
  • Engagement: To attend special worship services, guild activities, outreach programs, training workshops, and annual guild conferences.
  • Bible Study: To engage in Bible Study classes, aimed at fostering a deeper understanding of the Word of God.
  • Stewardship: To practice stewardship, nurturing a spirit of generosity and responsible management of resources.
