Anglican Women’s Fellowship (AWF)

Formation of the Organisation

The Anglican Women’s Fellowship (AWF) was conceptualized and established in the 1960s, specifically in 1964, through the key efforts of Archbishop Robert Selby Taylor. In collaboration with bishops from across the region, he worked tirelessly to convene the inaugural conference in Rosettenville, Johannesburg, in 1966. This conference brought together various stakeholders and church leaders to discuss the formation of a new women's organization aimed at developing and consolidating the work of women in the church. The primary goal was to create a comprehensive organization that all Anglican women could join, enhancing their roles and contributions.

The AWF was envisioned as a vital framework to connect various women’s organizations within the church, facilitating collaboration and fulfilling the dynamic role of women in the Anglican community. It was essential to note that the AWF was not intended to replace existing groups such as the Mothers’ Union; rather, it sought to harmonize and expand the participation of women, especially since the Mothers’ Union only admitted married women.

Today, the AWF is well-structured, with established handbooks and constitutions guiding its activities at all levels of the church. It attracts a diverse membership and continues to thrive, promoting worship and community service.

Specific Information About Guild History

Bishop Edward Fischer was instrumental in establishing the first AWF at St. Francis in Pretoria, appointing Clair Bezuidenhout as the inaugural president. Mrs. Lucy Motloi, previously a member of the Mothers’ Union, became one of the early members of the AWF following its launch.

Notable women who played significant roles in the Mamelodi parish include:

  • Ms. Hitty Maimane: The first chairperson of AWF in the parish
  • Ms. Florence Mkhombo: The second chairperson
  • Mrs. Morongwa Kekana: Secretary

Other contributing members include Mrs. Moila, Mrs. Meisie Kutumela, Mrs. Rhoda Mmusi, Mrs. Bessie Mohlamonayane, Mrs. Dorah Sebotsane, Mrs. Pauline Matloa, Mrs. Lekgothoane, Mrs. Clara Maifadi, Mrs. Mary Aphane, and Mrs. Mary Mokone.

Mrs. Motloi is recognized as the longest-serving member of the AWF, with other long-serving members including Mrs. Ellen Ngoasheng, Mrs. Elsie Chuene, Mrs. Josina Molefi, Mrs. Nomalungelo Sebotsane, Mrs. Sophie Ribane, Mrs. Fridah Tsekane, Mrs. Christine Ntoele, Mrs. Elizabeth Ntoele, Mrs. Winnie Makhambeni, Mrs. Talitha Mothapo, and Mrs. Khethiwe Chabalala.

Initially, the first AWF members comprised older women; however, the second cohort included four young women: Ms. Esther Motle, Ms. Johanna Motle, Ms. Lily Riba, and Ms. Moleko.

An interesting historical note shared by Ms. Matlakala Modise highlights that before the church building was constructed in Mamelodi, AWF members gathered at Denneboom Station, the current site of Tshwane Mall.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Anglican Women's Fellowship (AWF) is dedicated to supporting women in the church through prayer, fellowship, service, and community outreach, fostering spiritual growth and unity among its members.

You can join the AWF by reaching out to the Chairperson or any of the executive members listed above, or by attending one of our meetings or events for more information.

Membership is open to all Anglican women. There are no mandatory fees, but members may contribute voluntarily to support activities and events.
