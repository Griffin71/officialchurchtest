About AMG

The Anglican Men's Guild (AMG) was founded in the Parish of St. Francis Mamelodi West in 2004 by Mr. Moses Masemola, who introduced the concept of a unified men's guild to the parish. The guild began to take shape, with the establishment of uniform membership and operational policies.

Early Years

In 2005, the guild started to consider initiatives that would benefit men in the church. They also began to develop fundraising strategies, partnering with other Anglican parishes and eventually, other denominations, including Lutheran and African churches. This collaboration led to the creation of the Gauteng Men's Forum, a framework for men from various religions.

Recognition as a Diocesan Guild

The guild's goal was to achieve recognition as a Diocesan Guild. To achieve this, a constitution was written, and a motion was presented to Synod 2008. The guild was represented by St. Francis members, including Mr. Masemola, Abbey Thantsa, and Tati Makgoka.
